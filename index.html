What Makes a Good Map

Realistic Layout

Spaces need to make sense and every room should have a reason to exist. If you can't explain why something is there, it probably shouldn't be.

Lighting

Lighting is incredibly important in a map. It sets the overall feel for the entire map, if the lighting is bad, it completely ruins the immersion for players.

Roleplay Potential

A locked room, a messy desk or area, a hole in a wall leading to somewhere unexpected, all help make the players feel more immersed within the map, and helps create a story and an in-depth background to the map.

Optimization

If not one of the most important things, if the map has terrible performance, it not only ruins aspects such as combat and immersion, but the overall quality of the entire server.

Detail

Small things really add up to make the server feel truly realistic. Things like smudges on the wall, flickering lights, stains on the ground, all help create a background and a story to the environment.
